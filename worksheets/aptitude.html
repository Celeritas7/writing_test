Worked Example Solved Average speed

A person covers half the journey at 40 km/h and the other half at 20 km/h. Find the average speed for the whole journey.

GIVEN  equal distances d at 40 km/h and at 20 km/h
Approach Average speed = total distance / total time (NOT the simple average of the two speeds).
Work Let each half = d. time = d/40 + d/20 = d/40 + 2d/40 = 3d/40. total distance = 2d. avg = 2d ÷ (3d/40) = 80/3.
Answer average speed = 80/3 = 26.67 km/h
Check equals the harmonic mean 2·40·20 / (40+20) = 1600/60 = 26.67 ✓
